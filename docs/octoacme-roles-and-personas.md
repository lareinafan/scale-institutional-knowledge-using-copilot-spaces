# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Engineering Managers

### Role Summary
Engineering Managers ensure the delivery team has the people, focus, and operating rhythm needed to execute reliably. They support staffing, team health, and delivery accountability.

### Responsibilities
- Align capacity and staffing to project priorities
- Remove delivery blockers that require manager escalation
- Coach engineers on execution quality and collaboration
- Partner with PM/PdM on scope and timeline trade-offs
- Ensure sustainable delivery pace and incident readiness

### Goals
- Keep delivery predictable without burning out the team
- Improve team effectiveness and engineering quality
- Maintain clear accountability for commitments

### Typical Communication
- Weekly planning and status alignment with PM/PdM
- 1:1s and team syncs to address risks early
- Escalation and staffing updates to leadership

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Partners with Project Managers on schedule, dependencies, and risks
- Partners with Product Managers on scope and priority trade-offs
- Supports Developers with coaching, escalation, and execution clarity

---

## Design / UX Leads

### Role Summary
Design/UX Leads ensure solutions are usable, accessible, and aligned with customer workflows. They convert product goals into validated user experiences.

### Responsibilities
- Define UX direction, user flows, and interaction patterns
- Run or support user research and usability validation
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria for UX quality
- Provide design assets/specs for development
- Flag usability and accessibility risks early

### Goals
- Improve task success, adoption, and user satisfaction
- Reduce rework caused by unclear requirements
- Ensure consistent product experience quality

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Developers and Product Managers
- Research readouts and recommendation summaries
- Ongoing collaboration in planning and sprint reviews

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Partners with Product Managers to shape requirements and outcomes
- Works with Developers to ensure implementation fidelity
- Coordinates with Project Managers on timeline impact and readiness

---

## Technical Leads / Architects

### Role Summary
Technical Leads/Architects guide technical strategy and implementation patterns to ensure solutions are scalable, secure, and maintainable.

### Responsibilities
- Define architecture and technical standards for the initiative
- Break complex work into implementable technical milestones
- Review high-risk design decisions and dependencies
- Establish non-functional requirements (reliability, performance, security)
- Mentor developers on design and implementation approach

### Goals
- Reduce technical risk and long-term maintenance cost
- Improve system reliability and scalability
- Enable teams to deliver safely and quickly

### Typical Communication
- Technical design docs and architecture reviews
- Risk discussions in planning and delivery syncs
- PR feedback and implementation guidance

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Partners with Developers on solution design and code quality
- Partners with Project Managers on dependency mapping and sequencing
- Partners with Product Managers on technical trade-offs and feasibility

---

## Release Managers / Operations Coordinators

### Role Summary
Release Managers/Operations Coordinators drive release readiness and deployment coordination so production changes are predictable and low risk.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate release scope, cut dates, and readiness checks
- Validate release notes, rollout plans, and rollback procedures
- Confirm operational handoffs to support/on-call teams
- Track deployment risks and escalation paths
- Lead release communications before and after deployment

### Goals
- Reduce release-related incidents and rollbacks
- Improve confidence in deployment readiness
- Ensure stakeholders are informed of release impact

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews and go/no-go meetings
- Deployment status updates during release windows
- Post-release summaries with outcomes and follow-ups

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Works with Project Managers on release milestones and communication timing
- Works with Developers on deployment execution and technical validation
- Aligns with Product Managers on scope, customer impact, and launch messaging

---

## Customer Support / Support Readiness Leads

### Role Summary
Support Readiness Leads prepare customer support teams to handle new releases effectively, reducing customer friction and time to resolution.

### Responsibilities
- Translate release changes into support-facing guidance
- Prepare support playbooks, macros, and troubleshooting steps
- Identify common failure modes and escalation paths
- Coordinate support training ahead of launches
- Provide customer feedback trends back to product and project teams

### Goals
- Improve first-contact resolution and customer confidence
- Reduce support escalations after launches
- Strengthen feedback loops from customers to delivery teams

### Typical Communication
- Readiness checkpoints with release and project leads
- Launch briefings with support teams
- Post-release feedback summaries

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Partners with Release Managers for operational readiness
- Partners with Product Managers on customer-impact messaging
- Partners with Project Managers to track support readiness tasks

---

## Executive Sponsors

### Role Summary
Executive Sponsors provide strategic direction, funding support, and escalation authority to keep initiatives aligned with business priorities.

### Responsibilities
- Confirm strategic alignment and success outcomes
- Approve major scope/timeline trade-offs when needed
- Resolve high-impact cross-functional escalations
- Reinforce organizational support and decision clarity

### Goals
- Maximize business impact of project investments
- Remove organizational barriers to delivery
- Maintain alignment across leadership stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Milestone and monthly steering updates
- Decision-focused escalation reviews
- Sponsor updates at key phase gates

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Receives project/risk status from Project Managers
- Aligns on value outcomes and trade-offs with Product Managers
- Supports Engineering Managers during high-impact escalations

---

## Business Analysts / Operations Analysts

### Role Summary
Business/Operations Analysts translate business process needs into actionable requirements, helping teams implement measurable and operationally viable solutions.

### Responsibilities
- Document current-state and future-state workflows
- Define business rules, operational constraints, and reporting needs
- Support acceptance criteria quality and traceability
- Analyze delivery outcomes against target metrics
- Surface process adoption or change-management risks

### Goals
- Improve requirement clarity and implementation fit
- Increase operational effectiveness of delivered solutions
- Support data-driven refinement after release

### Typical Communication
- Requirement walkthroughs with Product and Delivery teams
- Process maps and metric reviews with stakeholders
- Operational readiness check-ins prior to release

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Partners with Product Managers on requirement clarity and metrics
- Partners with Developers on rule interpretation and edge cases
- Partners with Project Managers on scope impact and dependency tracking

---

## Security / Compliance Representatives

### Role Summary
Security/Compliance Representatives ensure projects meet required security, privacy, and regulatory expectations before and after release.

### Responsibilities
- Define security/compliance requirements early in planning
- Review architecture and implementation for policy alignment
- Coordinate risk assessments and required approvals
- Advise incident response obligations and communication requirements
- Track remediation requirements and follow-through

### Goals
- Reduce exposure to security and compliance risk
- Ensure readiness for audits and incident response
- Embed secure-by-default practices in delivery

### Typical Communication
- Security review checkpoints during planning/execution
- Risk and exception documentation with project owners
- Incident and remediation follow-up updates

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- Partners with Developers and Technical Leads on secure implementation
- Partners with Project Managers on risk tracking and escalation
- Partners with Product Managers on requirement and policy trade-offs
